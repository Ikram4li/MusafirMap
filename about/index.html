MusafirMap

MusafirMap helps Muslims determine travel boundaries according to Islamic rules.
This tool visualizes your home city and destination boundaries, showing whether your
destination falls within or outside the traveller's threshold (Ḥadd al-Safar).

Map

How to Use MusafirMap

1. Select Your Home City

In the "Home City" search box at the top, start typing your city name, full address, or postcode.

Suggestions will appear as you type. Click the correct one.

The map will display:

Black boundary → Your Home City Boundary

Blue buffer → Traveller's Threshold (Ḥadd al-Safar)

You can adjust the radius (in miles or kilometers) to change your travel boundary.

Alternatively - Use GPS to Detect Your Location

Your browser may ask for permission to access your location — allow it.
MusafirMap will automatically select your home city based on you GPS Location and show the boundaries and buffer zone of the city you are in.

2. Select a Destination

In the "Destination" search box, type the city name, address, or postcode of your destination.

Click the correct suggestion.

The map will display:

Green area → Destination outside your travel boundary

Red area → Destination overlapping your travel boundary

Both the home city boundary and destination boundaries will appear together for comparison.

3. Adjust Travel Buffer

The Defaullt Radius is set to 48 Miles as this is the most common Traveller Threshold Radius. However, if you follow a different opinion it can be changed. 

Use the radius input to change the size of your travel threshold.

Use the units selector to choose between miles and kilometers.

The map will update automatically to reflect changes.

Note: If unsure you should consult a scholar to for further advice

Understanding the Map

Below is the key which can also be seen when Navigating the Map.

Black Region : indicates the boundary of your home city. It is from this perimeter where the buffer distance Traveller's Threshold (Blue) is applied.

Blue Region : the Traveller's Threshold (Ḥadd al-Safar), there are multiple opinions on what this distance is but the more common opinions are 77km (48 Miles) or 88km (54 Miles). The Default value we use is 48 Miles. We encourage users to consult scholars if they are unsure what distance to use. 

Green Region : This is a region where you would be considered a Traveller (Musafir) as it exceeds the Traveller's Threshold (Ḥadd al-Safar). Here the rules of a traveller apply to you.

Red Region : This is a region where you would not be considered a Traveller (Musafir) as it exceeds the Traveller's Threshold (Ḥadd al-Safar). Here the rules of a traveller do not apply to you.

What is Ḥadd al-Safar?

Ḥadd al-Safar (حد السفر) is the Islamic travel distance threshold that determines when a Muslim is considered a traveler. When you travel beyond this boundary, certain Islamic rules apply, such as shortened prayers and exemption from fasting during Ramadan. Different schools of Islamic thought have different measurements for this threshold.

What distance is considered for the traveler's threshold?

The distance varies by Islamic school of thought. The most common measurements are approximately 48 miles (77 km) for Hanafi school, 56 miles (90 km) for Shafi'i and Hanbali schools, and 48-56 miles for Maliki school. MusafirMap allows you to adjust this distance according to your preferred school of thought.

How accurate are the boundaries shown on MusafirMap?

MusafirMap uses official administrative boundaries when available. For locations without defined boundaries, it creates a circular buffer around the central point. While we strive for accuracy, the map is intended as a guide rather than a definitive ruling. For religious matters, always consult with a knowledgeable scholar.

Can I save my home location for future use?

Currently, MusafirMap does not have a save feature. However, your browser may remember your last search if you allow it to store cookies. 

Is MusafirMap available as a mobile app?

MusafirMap is currently available as a web application optimized for both desktop and mobile browsers. You can add it to your home screen on most mobile devices for quick access. We're exploring the possibility of dedicated mobile apps for iOS and Android in the future.
